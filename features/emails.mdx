## Setup
For emails, we're using Mailjet. They provide an extremely generous free tier so you won't have to pay a dime for your MVP.

<Steps>
    <Step title="Register">
        Head over to <a href="https://www.mailjet.com/" target="_blank">Mailjet</a> and create an account (choose the free plan).
    </Step>
    <Step title="Get API Keys">
        Navigate to the API page and generate a secret key. Paste the API Key and Secret Key in your `.env` file.
        ```terminal .env
        MAILJET_API_KEY=[API KEY]
        MAILJET_SECRET_KEY=[SECRET KEY]
        ```
    </Step>
</Steps>

## Usage
You can use Mailjet to send emails programatically throughout your app. There is a usage demo in that you can reference in `src/routes/api/mailjet`. Click <a href="https://dev.mailjet.com/email/guides/send-api-v31/" target="_blank">here</a> to view the docs.

## Avoid the spam folder
To avoid having your emails go into spam, you will need authenticate your domain.
<Warning>You must have custom domain to do this.</Warning>

<Steps>
    <Step title="Add domain or sender address">
        Navigate to Mailjet and <a href="https://app.mailjet.com/account/sender?type=domain" target="_blank">add a domain or sender address</a>. Follow the steps to validate.
    </Step>
    <Step title="SPF/DKIM Setup">
        Go to the <a href="https://app.mailjet.com/account/sender?type=domain" target="_blank">SPF/DKIM Authentication</a> section, hit the cogwheel next to your domain and then click setup. 
    </Step>
    <Step title="Add Records">
        Your email service provider will have an admin panel where you can create and edit the SPF and DKIM records. Generally, you will need to create a new DKIM and edit the old SPF to include `include:spf.mailjet.com`.
    </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>Use subdomains for bulk email campaigns to uphold your domain reputation and avoid the spam folder.</Note>
